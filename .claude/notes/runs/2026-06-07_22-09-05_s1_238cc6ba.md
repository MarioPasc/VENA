## Concerns flagged (user notes)

### (3a) "Is the learning rate too small?" — YES, partially, but **the bigger bug is the scheduler.**

- `config.yaml:60-67`: `optim.lr: 5.0e-05`, `warmup_steps: 1000`, `scheduler: polynomial`.
- `config.yaml:76`: `training.total_steps: 1_000_000_000`. The polynomial scheduler decays to zero over `total_steps`; with a 1 e9 denominator and only ~1.18 e5 steps executed, **the scheduler is effectively constant** after warmup (`lr` ends at 4.999 e-5, identical to peak within rounding — see `train_step.csv` column `lr`).
- Whether 5 e-5 is small depends on what was tuned. For a trunk that is fully fine-tuned (FFT) from a public MAISI-V2 checkpoint *and* a freshly-initialised ControlNet, **5 e-5 is on the low side**: the MAISI-V2 paper uses 1 e-4 for its own joint trunk + CN training. A 2× LR (1 e-4) with the same 1000-step warmup is a reasonable first ablation.
- **Strong evidence that the loss landscape was actually steep at init** comes from grad-clip activity: **every step in the first 1000 hit `gradient_clip_val=1.0`** (preclip ≈ 8.8, postclip ≈ 1.0 — see `grad_norm_cn_preclip_mean` ratio 0.02× last/init). A higher peak LR would have made this worse.
- Fix priority: set `total_steps` to a realistic value (`max_epochs × steps_per_epoch ≈ 200 000`) so polynomial decay actually engages; consider warming up over 2 000 steps so the early clip saturation eases.

### (2) Trunk grad norm trend, S1 (FFT) — **matches CN closely; not alarming.**

In FFT, the trunk + CN share parameter scale (both billions of conv parameters). The two postclip series move together: 0.99 → 0.21 (CN), 0.99 → 0.20 (trunk). The plot in `plots/loss_total_grad.png` overlays them — they look identical at the screen scale. This is **what we expect for FFT**; the comparison with S2+LoRA (where trunk grad is 100× smaller because LoRA has 100× fewer parameters) is documented in the S2 note.

### Per-cohort CFM behaviour (additional flag — was not in user notes)

`plots/loss_per_cohort_grad.png` and `train_epoch.csv` `cfm_cohort_*_mean`:
- 5 of 7 cohorts hover around **CFM ≈ 1.3** (BraTS-GLI, IvyGAP, LUMIERE, REMBRANDT, UCSF-PDGM).
- **UPENN-GBM stays at CFM ≈ 2.1** through the entire run — ~60 % higher than the rest.
- Hypothesis: UPENN-GBM has different scanner / intensity statistics than the other cohorts, and the multi-cohort sampler (`tau=0.5`, `config.yaml:10`) puts only a fraction of its capacity there. Worth running a `tau=0.0` (uniform-by-cohort) ablation to test cohort imbalance.

## (5) Overall assessment of S1

- **Trained to a clean steady state.** CFM plateaus near epoch ~150–200 and barely moves after; from epoch 200 to 566 the CFM mean changes by < 0.01. The model has *converged on its current configuration*; running longer at 5 e-5 will not help.
- **Exhaustive-val PSNR at NFE=5 peaks at 27.24 dB / SSIM 0.921 (epoch 500)** — this is a competitive baseline for whole-volume T1c synthesis on multi-cohort 3-D data. **NFE=20 is *worse* than NFE=5 (26.49 dB / 0.919)**; that is unusual and suggests the rectified-flow trajectory has a sweet spot around NFE=5 rather than the more-NFE-is-better expectation. The Pareto plot makes this very visible.
- **NFE=1 collapses to SSIM 0.23 / PSNR 25.5 dB** — single-step sampling is unusable for this checkpoint. Plan accordingly when budgeting external-validation inference time.
- **No NaN events, no divergences** (no entries in module's NaN history).
- **Operational fixes** (high priority before the next 5-day Picasso run):
  1. Set `training.total_steps` to `max_epochs × steps_per_epoch` so polynomial decay actually decays.
  2. Try `optim.lr: 1.0e-04` (the MAISI-V2 default). If clip saturation worsens beyond the first 2 000 steps, raise `gradient_clip_val` to 2.0.
  3. Run a `tau=0.0` ablation to test whether UPENN-GBM lift is real cohort difficulty or a sampling artefact.

## (1) "Contrastive loss is negative — counter-intuitive, BG (Lp=3 outside brain) should fire up for vessels" — **the formula is doing exactly what it was designed to do; the user's mental model of the BG term is what's off.**

### Theory

The Lp-aware contrastive (`src/vena/model/fm/controlnet/losses/contrastive.py`) has two terms:

- **ROI (inside WT mask):** `loss_roi = -min(mean(|Δ|^p_t over m_wt), δ^p_t)`. The minus sign **pushes |Δ| up** inside the tumour (the ControlNet should differ from its mask-zeroed sibling there). The aggregate cap `δ^p_t = 2.0^1.0 = 2.0` upper-bounds the *mean of |Δ|^p_t* per sample.
- **BG (outside WT, in `m_bg`):** `loss_bg = mean(min(|Δ|^p_b, δ^p_b))` over `m_bg`. The per-voxel cap `δ^p_b = 2.0^3.0 = 8.0` upper-bounds each voxel's contribution. **The BG term does not care about vessels or contrast-captating tissue — it cares about `|Δ|`, which is `|v_orig - v_perturb|`, the difference between the original conditioning and the conditioning with the WT-mask channel zeroed.** Outside the tumour, the mask channel is already zero, so the perturbation is a no-op in that region; the network should produce the same velocity in both passes, so `|Δ| ≈ 0` in BG by construction.

Total per-sample contribution to the optimiser (after outer weight `contrastive_weight=0.01` in `config.yaml:69`):

`outer * (λ_roi · L_roi + λ_bg · L_bg) = 0.01 · (0.3 · (-min(mean|Δ|^1, 2.0)) + 1.0 · mean(min(|Δ|^3, 8.0)))`.

So **once the ROI hits the cap and BG stays small, the contrastive total is bounded near `-0.3 · 2.0 = -0.6` per sample**, which is exactly what we observe.

### Evidence from the aux diagnostics (the four `contrastive/*` columns we now log)

`train_step.csv`, mean over the first 200 / last 200 steps:

| Aux | Early | Late | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| `contrastive/delta_abs_mean_in` | 0.0116 | **3.3505** | Inside WT, `|Δ|` grew 290×. The model learned to make its mask-on and mask-off passes diverge inside the tumour — **exactly what ROI was supposed to push**. |
| `contrastive/delta_abs_mean_out` | 0.0111 | **0.0545** | Outside WT, `|Δ|` grew only 5× and stays tiny in absolute terms (≪ δ=2). The mask perturbation barely affects the network outside the tumour, as designed. |
| `contrastive/roi_cap_hit_frac` | 0.0000 | **0.9650** | 96.5 % of batches saturate the ROI cap. **The ROI term is no longer providing a learning signal late in training — it is a constant `-0.6`.** |
| `contrastive/bg_cap_hit_frac` | 0.0000 | **0.0001** | Effectively zero voxels in BG hit the per-voxel cap. The BG term contributes a tiny *positive* number (≈ 0.05). |

So `contrastive_mean ≈ 0.3 · (-2.0) + 1.0 · 0.05 = -0.55`, against the observed `-0.589`. The arithmetic matches.

### What the user's intuition missed

> "Lp=3 loss outside of the brain region, therefore, it should fire up even more for vessels and contrast-captating tissue that the model did not generate well."

The BG term **does not measure how well the model generated the target image**. It measures `|v_orig - v_perturb|` — the *velocity differential* between the original conditioning and the WT-mask-zeroed conditioning, in the same forward step. Vessels and enhancing tissue outside the tumour are not in `m_wt`, so zeroing the WT channel does not change their conditioning. The ControlNet sees identical inputs in both passes, produces nearly-identical velocities, and `|Δ| ≈ 0` regardless of generation quality. To get the behaviour the user expected (penalise poor synthesis of healthy / vascular regions), the loss would need access to the *target* velocity (CFM-style) over a vessel mask — that is the proposal §5.4 *vessel-conspicuity* loss, not the contrastive.

### Verdict

**The contrastive is working as specified; the negative sign is the steady-state.** Two design follow-ups worth considering:

1. **Saturation indicates the cap is too low or the outer weight is wrong.** `roi_cap_hit_frac = 0.965` means the ROI term is providing no gradient most of the time — it is a constant offset on the loss. Either raise δ from 2.0 (so the cap rarely saturates and ROI keeps pushing) or accept that ROI's only job was to get `delta_abs_mean_in` high enough and now it is done. Currently we are paying a 0.01 × 0.6 ≈ 0.006 constant drift on `total_mean` for nothing.
2. **Outer weight 0.01 is very low.** The contrastive contribution to `total` is `≈ -0.006`, vs CFM's `≈ 1.36`. Effectively the contrastive is sub-1 % of the gradient — much closer to a regulariser than a co-objective. If the goal was for it to shape the controlnet's behaviour, push it to 0.1; if the goal was a soft regulariser, the current weight is fine but acknowledge it as such in writeups.

## (2) Trunk grad norm trend — **explained, and *not* alarming.**

- **Absolute magnitudes are not comparable across regimes.** S1 (FFT): trunk has ~10⁹ trainable params; grad-norm starts at ~1.0. S2+LoRA: trunk has only the LoRA adapters (r=16 on Q,K,V,out_proj across all attention layers, ≈ 3 e6 params per the `decision.json` `trunk_peft_params`); grad-norm starts at ~0.004 because √(n_params) is two orders of magnitude smaller. The CN, in both regimes, has ~3 e8 freshly-initialised params; its grad-norm starts ~1.0 in S1 and ~0.5 in S2 (lower because S2 inherited the S1 trunk-only checkpoint, so CN at S2 init is not pure zero-init any more — see `runner/` resume logic).

- **The upward trend in the LoRA trunk grad-norm (0.0042 → 0.0054, +28 %) is the contrastive loss activating LoRA, not catastrophic forgetting.** Cross-reference with the aux table above: `contrastive/delta_abs_mean_in` grew 290× over the run. The mechanism producing `|Δ|` *is* the LoRA-conditioned trunk forward; the only way for `|Δ|` to grow is for the LoRA adapters to update. So the LoRA grads grow as the model learns to use the WT mask channel — the LoRA grad-norm trend is a *progress* indicator, not a damage indicator.

- **What would be alarming:** trunk grad-norm growing into the same scale as CN grad-norm (i.e. the LoRA is no longer LoRA — it is rewriting the trunk wholesale). Currently 0.0054 vs 0.348 — two orders of magnitude apart. No drift risk yet.

- **S1-vs-S2 fair comparison (per the user's question):** In S1 both trunk and CN postclip grad-norms move together (0.99 → 0.20) because they share parameter scale. In S2, CN grad-norm is `~0.35` and LoRA-trunk grad-norm is `~0.005`; the *per-parameter* gradient magnitudes are comparable (CN: 0.35 / √(3 e8) ≈ 2 e-5; LoRA: 0.005 / √(3 e6) ≈ 3 e-6). LoRA is being updated about 6× *less aggressively* per parameter than the CN, which is what we want for a parameter-efficient fine-tune.

## (5) Overall assessment of S2

- **CFM converged similarly to S1** (1.37 vs 1.32). The contrastive term modified the loss surface enough to push CFM 1 % higher at convergence — small price for the §5.3 regulariser.
- **Exhaustive-val is 1.29 dB worse than S1 at the same epoch (NFE=5).** `psnr_db: 25.95 vs 27.24`, `ssim: 0.908 vs 0.921`. This is **the most important number in this whole report**: the §5.3 contrastive, with the chosen δ and λs, hurt synthesis quality. Possible causes (ranked by my prior):
  1. **ROI cap saturation** removes useful gradient and trades it for a constant offset — the ROI term shapes the network early then *parks* it in a slightly-suboptimal CFM basin. δ=2 may be too low, or λ_roi=0.3 too high once `delta_abs_mean_in` is already deep above δ.
  2. **`contrastive_weight=0.01` is *too small* to be useful but *just large enough* to perturb the gradient direction.** It changes the optimum without contributing meaningfully to the model's capacity to differentiate mask-on/mask-off. Either ramp it up (0.1) so it actually does work, or zero it out and only use it as a diagnostic.
  3. **LoRA r=16 is the new bottleneck.** S1 had full trunk capacity; S2 traded that for adapter slots. Worth testing r=32 with `target_modules` also including FFN (`to_out` for MAISI-V2 attention blocks) to give the LoRA more headroom.
- **Trunk grad-norm trend is healthy** (see Q2). LoRA is updating; not catastrophically forgetting.
- **No NaN events.**
- **Operational fixes** (high priority):
  1. **Schedule fix** (shared with S1): set `training.total_steps` properly so polynomial decay engages.
  2. **Contrastive ablation matrix is needed before the next 5-day Picasso.** Three runs:
     - `contrastive_weight=0.0` (= pure S1 with LoRA-only trunk) — disentangles the contribution of LoRA vs contrastive.
     - `contrastive_weight=0.1, δ=3.0` (more headroom for the ROI term, more impact on `total`).
     - `contrastive_weight=0.0` with FFT trunk (= S1 control, for a fair LoRA-vs-FFT comparison without the contrastive confound).
  3. **Consider replacing the contrastive ROI term with a target-aware term** (proposal §5.4 vessel-conspicuity) — the current contrastive does what it says, but what the user *wanted* (penalise poor vessel synthesis) needs the target, not the perturbed conditioning.
